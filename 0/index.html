Project 0 — Focal Length, Perspective & Dolly Zoom

CS180/CS280A • Photography & Perception

Part 1 — Selfie: The Wrong Way vs. The Right Way

Two portraits framed to the same face size. The first is a close, wide‑angle selfie; the second is shot from farther away with more zoom to reduce perspective distortion.

Why it looks better: perspective is set by camera distance, not focal length. Stepping back equalizes distances to facial features; zoom just matches framing.

Part 2 — Architectural Perspective Compression

Same scene, two distances. The far+zoomed shot appears flatter (compressed), while the close+wide shot feels deeper with stronger converging lines.

Why it looks different: From far away, the depth range within frame is smaller, so relative size changes with distance are muted (compression). Up close, those differences grow.

Part 3 — The Dolly Zoom

Camera moves backward while zooming in, keeping the subject size roughly constant. Combine 4–8+ frames into a GIF to reveal the background warping.
